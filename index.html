Understanding the scale

How does this work?

The Fahrenheit temperature scale was originally proposed to reflect the average human body temperature at 96 °F and the freezing point of water at 32 °F. With the Fahrenheit scale, weather temperatures are relatively intuitive for everyday use, but the is not ideal for scientific applications. While the Celsius scale is more scientifically useful, its weather temperatures fall in a range that is less intuitive for everyday use - for example, in Los Angeles, the average high temperature in August is 29 °C, and the average low temperature in December is 9 °C. This range of just 20 degrees can make it harder to quickly assess how hot or cold the weather feels.
